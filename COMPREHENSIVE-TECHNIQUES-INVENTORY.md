## 1. Materials & Materials Preparation

### Copper Wire Working
**Projects:** English Country Garden, Traditional English Cottage, Log Cabin, Plant Displays  
**Description:** Recovery of copper wire from electrical sources (wire stripping, capacitor leads, coil windings). Pre-coating with acrylic paint in various colors to hide copper gloss. Wire sorted and bundled by diameter (ultra-fine <0.1mm, fine 0.3-0.5mm, medium 0.8-1.2mm, heavy up to 2mm).  
**Documentation Status:** Well-documented with multiple applications

### Paper Stock Preparation
**Projects:** English Country Garden, Traditional English Cottage, Plant Displays  
**Description:** Multiple weights of paper (80gsm printer paper, 17-35gsm tissue, 160-220gsm cardstock) pre-colored with acrylics in range of colors, dried and filed for easy access. Used for petals, leaves, templates, and structural parts.  
**Documentation Status:** Well-documented

### Natural Materials Collection & Preservation
**Projects:** Log Cabin, English Country Garden, Traditional English Cottage  
**Description:** Collection of twigs, dried plant roots, seed heads, dried moss, grasses, sphagnum moss from woodland areas and garden prunings. Materials air-dried in dark location, sealed with diluted PVA, treated with vinegar to suppress mold. Sorted by texture, color, and size.  
**Documentation Status:** Well-documented

### Sawdust Processing
**Projects:** Log Cabin, English Country Garden, Traditional English Cottage  
**Description:** Sawdust sifted into different grades (fine, medium, coarse), then colored with acrylics in greens, whites, pinks, reds, browns. Used for foliage texture, ground cover, and tree covering.  
**Documentation Status:** Well-documented

### Bamboo Preparation
**Projects:** Traditional English Cottage, Bamboo River Lookout, Log Cabin  
**Description:** Mature and dried bamboo cut with band saw into strips (5mm typical), hand-planed to square posts or thinner strips. Various diameters sorted for different applications. Can be beveled, drilled, shaped.  
**Documentation Status:** Well-documented with fence, trellis, and structure applications

### Slate Crushing & Sifting
**Projects:** Traditional English Cottage  
**Description:** Welsh slate crushed and sifted to separate into fine and medium-coarse grains. Fine mixed with Mod Podge for tarmac surface, coarse mixed with fine sand for gravel paths.  
**Documentation Status:** Mentioned with application details

### Tea Leaves for Ground Cover
**Projects:** Log Cabin  
**Description:** Used tea leaves as fine-textured ground cover material, colored with paint, mixed with other materials for forest floor effect.  
**Documentation Status:** Mentioned only

### Sisal Preparation from Rope
**Projects:** Log Cabin  
**Description:** Sisal untwined from rope, colored, cut into short pieces for pine needles and grass-like effects.  
**Documentation Status:** Mentioned with application

### Wood Strips from Garden Trees
**Projects:** Log Cabin, Traditional English Cottage  
**Description:** Garden tree branches (Acer, Bay leaves) properly dried and cut with table saw into strips of different thicknesses (2mm typical) and widths. Used for cabin construction, cottage framing, details.

## 2. Structural Construction Techniques

### Wood Frame with Paper Cladding
**Projects:** Traditional English Cottage  
**Description:** Thin wood slats (~2mm) sandwiched between two sheets of paper with ribs added to prevent sagging. Each wall measured and formed separately with windows/doors cut out. Provides structural rigidity with better bonding surface for modeling paste.  
**Documentation Status:** Well-documented with measurements and assembly process

### Log Construction (Cabin Walls)
**Projects:** Log Cabin  
**Description:** Individual wall frames built separately, then fitted with wood strips to simulate logs. Each log individually cut, stained with color variation, notched at corners for authentic interlocking appearance. Walls assembled with corner notching aligned.  
**Documentation Status:** Well-documented with detailed process

### Carved Wooden Hull Construction
**Projects:** SS Great Britain, Sea Rescue  
**Description:** Hull carved from basswood block using profile drawings. Rough cut with craft knife from centerline outward, sanded with progressively finer grades for smooth curves. Sealed with diluted PVA before painting. Frequent measuring with calipers for symmetry.  
**Documentation Status:** Well-documented in build logs

### Balsa Wood Layering Technique
**Projects:** Caravaning (Caravan), SS Great Britain  
**Description:** Multiple layers of balsa wood cut and spaced to achieve correct heights. Used for vehicle bodies and hull structures. Provides lightweight core with good shaping potential.  
**Documentation Status:** Well-documented

### Beer Can Metal Hull Plating
**Projects:** SS Great Britain  
**Description:** Beer cans used for thin steel/aluminum sheet metal. Strips cut and glued with superglue over balsa wood hull core filled with sawdust and glue mixture. Creates authentic iron-hull appearance.  
**Documentation Status:** Well-documented with photos

### Styrene Sheet Construction
**Projects:** Sea Rescue, Caravaning  
**Description:** 0.5mm and 1mm styrene sheet for superstructures. Cut slightly oversize, test fit before gluing with liquid poly cement. Squared up with set square during assembly. Score multiple passes for clean cuts.  
**Documentation Status:** Well-documented

### Air-Dry Clay Body Forming
**Projects:** Caravaning (Car)  
**Description:** Air-dry clay applied over internal armature (balsa core, wire reinforcement, lightweight filler). Extended working time allows gradual shaping. Can be carved at leather-hard stage. Sands well when fully dry.  
**Documentation Status:** Well-documented including failures and learning curve

### Epoxy Putty (Milliput) Sculpting
**Projects:** Log Cabin (Chimney), Caravaning (Caravan body, details)  
**Description:** Two-part epoxy putty mixed and applied over wood cores. Can be shaped, textured while workable. Files and sands after hardening. Used for chimneys, vehicle bodies, curved surfaces. Limited working time requires fast work.  
**Documentation Status:** Well-documented with advantages and challenges noted

### Bamboo Structural Systems
**Projects:** Bamboo River Lookout, Log Cabin (patio)  
**Description:** Heavy paper sheets as structural basis with bamboo sticks glued to form walls, floor, roof, patio. Thicker bamboo pieces as internal uprights for structural strength. Drill and glue technique for connections.  
**Documentation Status:** Well-documented

### Multi-Faceted Roof Construction
**Projects:** Traditional English Cottage  
**Description:** Each roof facade modeled separately with paper, fitted around chimney. Ridges made from double-folded paper with V-shape fitted over roof peaks. Zig-zag scissors for decorative edge.  
**Documentation Status:** Well-documented

## 3. Plant & Botanical Techniques

### Copper Wire Tree Construction
**Projects:** Traditional English Cottage, English Country Garden  
**Description:** Main branches from thick copper wire, thickened and tapered with hot glue gun. Thinner branches individually twisted and fixed with hot glue. Spray painted bark colors. Tree mounted on jig for leaf application. Can take weeks to complete.  
**Documentation Status:** Well-documented with detailed process and time investment

### Stencil-Cut Leaves
**Projects:** Traditional English Cottage, English Country Garden  
**Description:** Paper pre-painted in greens, cut with leaf stencils in various shapes. Individual leaves glued to wire stems, then bundled by twisting wires together or setting in hot glue. Used for hostas, ferns, large-leaved plants. Multiple sheets (3+ A4) needed for single tree.  
**Documentation Status:** Well-documented with applications and quantities

### Micro Cup Flowers
**Projects:** English Country Garden  
**Description:** Leather hole cutting tool used to punch micro cups from paper. Small ball sculpting tool forms cup shape. Glued individually to green wire stems. Suitable for small bell-shaped flowers.  
**Documentation Status:** Well-documented with tool specifications

### Clay-Molded Flowers

## 4. Figure Making & Sculpting

### Wire Armature Figure Construction
**Projects:** Caravaning, Bamboo River Lookout  
**Description:** Twisted wire skeletal armature with proportions marked for head, torso, limbs. Provides stability for clay application. Custom holder created to support armature during delicate clay work. Most promising technique for future development.  
**Documentation Status:** Well-documented including failures and learning curve

### Air-Dry Clay Figure Sculpting
**Projects:** Caravaning, Bamboo River Lookout  
**Description:** Air-dry clay layered onto wire armature, shaped for body forms. Extended working time compared to epoxy putty but challenges at tiny scale include shrinkage, surface texture, gravity issues on extended limbs. Failed technique at small scale for this builder.  
**Documentation Status:** Well-documented including detailed failures

### Epoxy Putty Figure Sculpting
**Projects:** Log Cabin (Fisherman)  
**Description:** Milliput two-part epoxy putty formed into first figure attempt (seated fisherman). Very difficult to work with - arms and legs kept breaking off, fine details impossible at small scale. Limited working time adds pressure.  
**Documentation Status:** Well-documented with honest assessment of challenges

### Carved Wooden Figures
**Projects:** Caravaning  
**Description:** Simplified stylized figures carved from hardwood (basswood/lime). Basic human silhouette carved with minimal detail - proportions correct, posture characteristic, paint defines clothing and features. Suggestion beats precision at viewing distance. Head 1/7 height, torso 3/7, legs 3/7.  
**Documentation Status:** Well-documented as successful pivot from clay failures

### Dog/Animal Carving from Wood
**Projects:** Caravaning (Tilly the dog)  
**Description:** Light-colored hardwood carved for small dog figure (~10mm). Profile drawn on block, rough shape cut with coping saw, detail carving with craft knife and scalpel, needle files for refining, 600-grit sanding for smoothing. Crisp edges, no shrinkage, details stay in place. Breakthrough technique.  
**Documentation Status:** Extremely well-documented with detailed process and success story

### Figure Painting for Character
**Projects:** Caravaning, Bamboo River Lookout  
**Description:** Paint defines clothing through color blocks, suggests features (eyes as dark dots), creates hair with color and texture, defines flesh tones minimally. Thin layers preserve carved detail, distinct color blocks read better at scale, matte finish for fabric.  
**Documentation Status:** Well-documented

### Plastic Figure Painting
**Projects:** Traditional English Cottage  
**Description:** Commercial plastic figures used and painted for scale reference and population.  
**Documentation Status:** Mentioned only

## 6. Landscape & Terrain Building

### Homemade Clay for Terrain
**Projects:** Log Cabin, English Country Garden, Traditional English Cottage  
**Description:** Flour, PVA glue, water, vinegar (suppress mold), cooking oil (reduce stickiness) mixed to paste. Applied in layers over styrofoam base. Issues with cracking in early attempts - more PVA, less water improved results. Can take 2+ weeks to fully dry and stabilize.  
**Documentation Status:** Well-documented with failures and improvements noted

### Layered Ground Texturing
**Projects:** Log Cabin  
**Description:** Layer 1: Diluted PVA brushed on, fine sand/soil mixture sprinkled wet, multiple coats. Layer 2: Coarser sand and gravel in paths, fine turf in grass areas, darker earth near structures. Layer 3: Organic materials from sorted collection matched by texture and color.  
**Documentation Status:** Well-documented with three-layer system

### Crushed Slate for Tarmac/Roads
**Projects:** Traditional English Cottage  
**Description:** Laminate floor underlay sealed with Mod Podge for road base. Fine crushed slate mixed with Mod Podge and pasted on. Coarser stone spread on surface in areas. Road base glued to model base.  
**Documentation Status:** Well-documented

### Gravel Path Construction
**Projects:** Traditional English Cottage, English Country Garden  
**Description:** Floor underlay strips sealed with Mod Podge, coarse crushed slate mixed with fine sand and Mod Podge to form paste, spread thinly with paint enhancement. Real slate crushed for pathways with filler for edges.  
**Documentation Status:** Well-documented

### Stone Foundation Wall
**Projects:** Log Cabin  
**Description:** Horticultural grit stones individually glued to level cabin and create foundation. Weathered with paint, plants added in crevices for realism.  
**Documentation Status:** Well-documented

### Cement Edge/Step Construction

**Documentation Status:** Well-documented

### Static Grass Lawn Application

### Polystyrene/Styrofoam Base Structure

## 11. Problem-Solving Approaches & Philosophy
