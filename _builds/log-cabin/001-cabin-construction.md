## Project Start

Started the Log Cabin in the Woods project on January 20, 2024. This scratch-build diorama focuses on creating an authentic rustic cabin from individual components, set within a natural woodland environment at 1:35 scale.

## Planning and Materials

Initial planning involved:
- Researching authentic log cabin construction techniques
- Gathering reference images of traditional cabin architecture
- Sourcing appropriate materials for scaled logs
- Planning the cabin dimensions and layout
- Sketching the basic structure

Key materials for cabin construction:
- Basswood strips and dowels for logs
- Balsa wood for roof structure
- Stone-textured material for chimney
- Fine sandpaper for weathering
- Acrylic paints in earth tones
- Wood stains and washes

Building the walls required patience and precision. Each log was:
- Cut to length with slight variations for realism
- Bark texture carved along the length
- Edges chamfered slightly for weathered appearance
- Stained with various wood tones for natural variation
- Fitted and glued in place with corner notches aligned

## Build Reflections

### What Worked Well
- Individual log construction gave authentic appearance
- Corner notching technique created realistic joints
- Variation in wood tones added natural character
- Roof structure method was sturdy and realistic

### Challenges Encountered
- Maintaining consistent log diameter while adding texture
- Aligning corner notches precisely for tight fit
- Keeping walls level as height increased
- Individual shingles were time-intensive but worth the effort

### Skills Developed
- Fine detail carving for bark texture
- Scale woodworking techniques
- Building structural elements from scratch
- Weathering and aging wood surfaces

## Time Investment

**Total cabin construction time:** Approximately 40-45 hours
- Planning and material prep: 5 hours
- Foundation and base logs: 8 hours
- Wall construction: 18 hours
- Roof structure and shingles: 12 hours
- Chimney and details: 6 hours
