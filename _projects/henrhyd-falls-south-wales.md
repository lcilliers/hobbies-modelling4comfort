## Project Overview

A dramatic diorama capturing the natural beauty of Henrhyd Falls, the tallest waterfall in the Brecon Beacons National Park, South Wales.

## Description

This project will recreate the stunning 90-foot drop of Henrhyd Falls, surrounded by lush woodland and dramatic rock formations. The falls gained additional fame as a filming location for the Batcave entrance in "The Dark Knight Rises."

## Build Goals

- Create realistic waterfall effects
- Develop authentic Welsh woodland environment
- Capture the dramatic scale of the falls
- Incorporate realistic rock formations and moss
- Practice water effect techniques

## Challenges

- Achieving convincing water flow effects
- Creating realistic vertical cliff faces
- Scale representation of dramatic height
- Woodland vegetation at appropriate scale
- Maintaining structural integrity with water features

## Techniques to Explore

- Water effect techniques (resin, gloss medium, clear silicone)
- Rock carving and texturing
- Vertical terrain construction
- Moss and lichen effects
- Woodland vegetation techniques
- Atmospheric effects (mist, spray)

## Historical Context

Henrhyd Falls is located on the Nant Llech river near Coelbren in the Brecon Beacons. The falls drop 90 feet (27 meters) into a wooded gorge, making it the highest waterfall in South Wales. The site is managed by the National Trust and is a popular destination for hikers.
