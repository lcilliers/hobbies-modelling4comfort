## Description

This project will recreate the timeless charm of a traditional English cottage, complete with thatched roof, Cotswold stone walls, leaded windows, and surrounding cottage garden. The build will focus on authentic architectural details and weathering to capture the aged beauty of rural English vernacular architecture.

## Build Goals

- Create authentic thatched roof texture and appearance
- Build realistic stone wall construction
- Develop period-appropriate windows and doors
- Incorporate authentic architectural details (chimneys, dormers, timbers)
- Add surrounding cottage garden elements
- Practice advanced weathering and aging techniques
- Achieve realistic scale for traditional cottage proportions

## Challenges

- Thatched roof texture and layering

- Leaded window detail at scale
- Realistic aging and weathering
- Period-appropriate color palette

- Integration with landscape elements

**Typical Elements:**
- Thatched or stone tile roof

## Historical Context

Traditional English cottages evolved from medieval peasant dwellings, developing regional characteristics based on local materials. Cotswold cottages are known for honey-colored limestone, while others feature timber framing, cob walls, or brick construction. Thatched roofs remain iconic, though many were replaced with tiles over time.

## Regional Styles to Consider

- **Cotswold:** Honey-colored stone, stone tile roofs
- **Tudor:** Timber framing, whitewashed infill
- **West Country:** Cob walls, thatched roofs
- **East Anglia:** Flint and brick, pantile roofs
